Virtual reality has the power to transport viewers to distant planets and faraway lands.Now we’re using it to share a more significant alternate reality —

immersing viewers in the everyday lives of people struggling to survive all over the world. Because we believe immersive storytelling leads to empathy.

OUR MISSION
WE USE THE POWER OF IMMERSIVE STORYTELLING TO CREATE POSITIVE SOCIAL CHANGE.
Long the purview of the gaming world, virtual reality represents a new frontier for journalism.
– The New York Times

In January 2015, we debuted “Clouds over Sidra,” a 9-minute Virtual Reality film that took United Nations leaders on an unfiltered tour of the Zaatari Syrian refugee camp in northwest Jordan—without ever leaving the World Economic Forum at Davos. The story of 12-year-old Sidra and her indomitable spirit lead to the United Nations.

And that’s just the start. We’ve also discovered that Unicef donation [Contextual link to unicef donation page?] rates increased 60% after viewing the film—a dramatic increase in awareness, participation, and funding that’s desperately needed.

Still, there’s so much more to do.

We’ve just released our second film, “Waves of Grace,” the triumphant story of an Ebola survivor in Liberia. And we have five more immersive stories of the lives of the world’s vulnerable in production.
